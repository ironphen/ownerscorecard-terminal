# Accounting-logic audit — confirmed findings

> Commissioned audit of the Owner Scorecard accounting logic across the US, Japan and ADR
> pools for soundness against Graham / Buffett / Munger principles. 36 findings confirmed
> of 45 raised (6 rejected on adversarial verification). Each was reproduced
> from real data by an independent verifier before inclusion.

**Severity:** 18 high · 8 medium · 10 low

The dominant root cause (Theme A) is the same defect in many places: a verdict is published
on data that is missing or implausible, instead of being withheld — the very fiction
debtReliable() already prevents for debt. Most fixes are one guard applied in N spots.

## Theme A. Missing believability guards (false verdicts on bad/missing data)

_10 finding(s): 7 high, 1 medium, 2 low._

### 1. [HIGH] Incremental ROIC produces implausible 100–360% figures labeled 'good' / 'still compounding' — small-denominator artifacts presented as moat verdicts

- **Dimension / pools:** moat-roic · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Buffett's one-dollar test on incremental capital is meant to separate a compounding moat from one being milked; a 323% 'return on reinvested capital' is not a credible economic statement and pronounces a verdict the data cannot support ('present never pronounce').
- **What is wrong:** The centerpiece fact computes inc = dNOPAT / dInvested over avg-first-3 vs avg-last-3 years. The only guard is dInv > ivE*0.1 (10% growth in the invested base). For asset-light software firms the invested base is tiny, so a small NOPAT change yields an enormous ratio; for heavy buyback names equity (and thus invested capital) is suppressed, shrinking the denominator. The result is shown with tone 'good' and the note 'Every extra dollar the company reinvested earned a high return, it is still compounding, not coasting on an old moat.'
- **Evidence:** Confirmed: src/lib/durability.mjs lines 76-92; invested() line 26-30 floors totalDebt+equity-cash to >0; guard is dInv>ivE*0.1; 'good' at inc>=0.15. Reproduced on real US data: APPF inc=323% (ivE $0.100B, ivL $0.126B, dInv $0.026B, dNOPAT $0.083B), APP 303%, COCO 368% (dInv $0.009B just clears guard), AZO 170% with FY2024 equity -$4.75B and window-avg equity -$4.17B floored to a positive $3-5B invested base. Pool-wide: 51 companies inc>100% labeled 'good' (matches claim), 16 inc>200% (matches claim), 52 of shown companies have negative avg ending equity. One correction: I counted 105 companies with inc>60% (not the claimed 156) under debtReliable filtering; the load-bearing >100%-good (51) and >200% (16) counts match exactly, so the discrepancy does not affect the verdict.
- **Suggested fix:** Tighten the guard so the denominator is economically meaningful: require dInv to exceed an absolute floor and a larger fraction of ivE (e.g. >30%), and cap/withhold the figure when |inc| is implausible (>~50–60%) or when equity is negative over the window. When the denominator is dominated by buybacks (equity fell while invested barely moved), route to the 'returns capital' message instead of a triple-digit compounding claim.

### 2. [HIGH] Buyback average price published with no plausibility guard — garbage repurchasedShares yields $0.00/$5,482 prices presented as fact (~4% of US names)

- **Dimension / pools:** capital-allocation · US
- **Principle:** Present never pronounce; every figure traceable and shown with its honest limit; Buffett — buybacks only good below value, so the price paid is the whole point
- **What is wrong:** avgBuybackPrice = total buyback dollars ÷ split-adjusted repurchasedShares. The repurchasedShares field in the data is heavily polluted (dollar amounts, scaling errors, partials), often orders of magnitude larger than the company's entire diluted share count. The bbPriceUnreliable guard ONLY fires when a clean stock split is detected (line 143: `if (splitMultiples.length)`), so for the overwhelming majority of names — those with no split — there is NO sanity check at all. There is no test that repShares ≤ sharesDiluted, nor that the implied price is within a sane band of a real stock price. The card then states, as a hard fact, 'X M shares were bought for $Y, about $0.00 each.'
- **Evidence:** All four cited tickers reproduce (USD pool, capitalHistory run on real data): AME — raw repShares per year are 7.1B, 114B, 5.1B, 133B, 55B, 113B, 2.7B, 100B, 1.3 trillion, 2.3 trillion against ~232M diluted shares; blended avgBuybackPrice = $0.00042/share, bbPriceUnreliable=false (AME trades ~$170). ICE — repShares include 903B, 13.6 trillion, 4.96 trillion, 7.7 trillion vs ~575M diluted; avgBuybackPrice = $0.00024/share, unreliable=false. POOL — repShares 2.06B/1.35B/1.29B vs ~42M diluted; avgBuybackPrice = $0.109/share, unreliable=false (POOL trades ~$350). AM — single year repShares=22,896 (far too small) with $125.5M spent; avgBuybackPrice = $5,482/share, unreliable=false. Population: 965 names publish a price, 39 (4.0%) implausible (<$2 or >$2000); bbPriceUnreliable=true count across the entire US pool = 0; 30 companies have a year with repShares > sharesDiluted (impossible). The original finding's numbers are accurate. Note: the rendered value is rounded by per()/the formatter, so it surfaces as $0.00 to the user, but the underlying computed figure is e.g. $0.00042 (AME), $0.11 (POOL), $5,482 (AM).
- **Suggested fix:** Before publishing avgBuybackPrice, reject any year whose repShares exceeds the diluted share count (a company cannot repurchase more shares than exist) and reject the blended figure if the implied price is wildly out of band (e.g. price < $1 or price/sharePrice ratio absurd). Apply the per-year-reconciliation/withhold logic regardless of whether a split was detected, not only inside `if (splitMultiples.length)`.

### 3. [HIGH] Dividend record asserts 'Paid no dividend over the span' when dividendsPaid is merely missing from the data — false statement for known dividend payers (JNJ)

- **Dimension / pools:** capital-allocation · US, ADR, Japan
- **Principle:** Present never pronounce; each figure traceable to a filing; never assert a fact the data does not support
- **What is wrong:** The card derives divYears/dpsGrowth/everCut purely from L.dividendsPaid. When that line is absent from the extracted data, divYears=0 and dpsLast=null, and the component (Capital.astro line 64-67) renders value 'None' and the categorical note 'Paid no dividend over the span; it returns cash through buybacks or retains it.' This is an affirmative false statement, not an honest 'unknown' — it conflates 'we have no dividend line' with 'the company pays no dividend.'
- **Evidence:** Confirmed on JNJ in fundamentals.json: dividendsPaid is null in every history year (FY2017,2018,2019,2021,2022,2023,2024,2025) and in ttm.lines. capitalHistory(JNJ) returns divYears=0, dpsFirst=null, dpsLast=null, dpsGrowth=null, div=0. Card renders value "None" and note "Paid no dividend over the span; it returns cash through buybacks or retains it." Prevalence in this dataset snapshot: 671 of 1894 carded US companies have dividendsPaid absent (null) in every span year; 313 carry at least one present, zero value (so present-vs-absent is distinguishable). The finding's exact counts (933/2202) differ from this snapshot but the mechanism and the JNJ proof case are exact. Other famous payers (KO, PG, PEP, XOM, CVX, IBM, MMM, MCD) DO have the line and label correctly, and ADBE is a genuine non-payer correctly shown as "None" — so the bug is the silent absent-line case, with JNJ as the clear counterexample.
- **Suggested fix:** Distinguish 'dividendsPaid line absent across the span' (show '—' / 'Not reported in the data') from 'dividendsPaid present and zero' (show 'None'). Only assert 'Paid no dividend' when at least one year carries a present, zero value.

### 4. [HIGH] Leverage-creep check has no debtReliable guard, so it publishes a reassuring false-clear on companies whose debt is grossly under-captured

- **Dimension / pools:** inversion · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Munger inversion ('did debt outgrow the business?'); the rest of the codebase already declines debt reads when capture is unreliable
- **What is wrong:** Test 3 compares startL.totalDebt to endL.totalDebt with no call to debtReliable() (which the ROIC path uses). For financing-arm filers whose debt is tagged only under segment dimensions the companyfacts API strips, totalDebt collapses to near-zero, so the check sees debt 'fall' and prints the clean note 'borrowing did not run ahead of what the business earns' — actively reassuring the reader on broken data.
- **Evidence:** Ford (F, US): history totalDebt = 142.97B (FY2016) -> 471M (TTM, stale FY2020 substitute; FY2021-2025 are null). Leverage check renders value '$143.0B -> $471M', flagged=false, note: 'borrowing did not run ahead of what the business earns.' debtReliable(startL)=true, debtReliable(endL)=false (interestExpense 1.316B / totalDebt 471M = 2.79, far above the 0.5 ceiling). Code: inversion.mjs lines 75-76 gate only on d0/d1 being non-null and >=0; no debtReliable call. Contrast Portrait.astro line 14 which gates debt reads on debtReliable of both lines and ttm.lines. Pool-wide: 28 US companies get a leverage false-clear under debtReliable-false conditions vs 7 flagged. Original finding's numbers and reasoning are accurate.
- **Suggested fix:** Guard the leverage check with debtReliable(startL) && debtReliable(endL); when either is false, withhold the check rather than show a 'debt fell' clear. Also consider rejecting the comparison when one endpoint's totalDebt is null and a stale fallback is being substituted.

### 5. [HIGH] Debt under-capture (Ford/AES/Textron + ~47 ADRs) shown as 'Net cash, debt-free' by cashPosition() and 'Conservative' by leverage() — the exact fiction debtReliable() exists to prevent

- **Dimension / pools:** solvency · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Graham — solvency first; most permanent losses come from leverage, not bad businesses. The tool's own rule: every figure honest, never paint a debt-laden company as net cash.
- **What is wrong:** debtReliable(L) (fundamentals.mjs:501) detects when a company's interest bill implies far more debt than its filings tag (interest/totalDebt > 0.5, or interest>1e8 with null/zero debt). roic() and roicValue() correctly gate on it and decline ('Debt under-captured'). But cashPosition() (net-debt headline) and leverage() do NOT call debtReliable. So the same company simultaneously reports, in one 'Will it survive?' scorecard: roic='Debt under-captured, cannot be read honestly', but cashPosition='Net cash, debt-free' and leverage='0.1x Conservative'. This is a direct internal contradiction that overstates solvency on the most leveraged names in the universe.
- **Evidence:** cashPosition() (fundamentals.mjs:203) and leverage() (168) do not call debtReliable(L), while roic() (261) does — and all three render in the same scorecard (leverage at line 627, cashPosition at 628, roic at 635). Verified contradictions: BABA (ADR): totalDebt CN¥6.028B, interest CN¥9.793B, debtReliable=false → leverage "0.1× Conservative" + cashPosition "+CN¥280.8B Net cash", yet roic "Debt under-captured". BN: leverage "1.2× Conservative", cashPosition "+$7.3B Net cash", roic "Debt under-captured". ABEV: "0.1× Conservative" / "+R$18.1B Net cash". ACN: "0.0× Conservative" / "+$10.1B Net cash". US pool: Ford totalDebt $471M vs interest $1.254B → cashPosition "+$38.0B Net cash" (but leverage actually reads "Debt against an operating loss" because OI is −$9.169B this year, NOT "0.1x Conservative" as the finding stated); AES (totalDebt null, interest $1.407B) → cashPosition "+$1.6B Net cash, debt-free", leverage returns null; Textron (null / $142M) → "+$1.3B Net cash, debt-free", leverage null. ADR-pool scan (non-financial): 49 names flagged debt-under-captured yet labelled "Net cash" by cashPosition (finding said 47), 18 of which also read leverage "Conservative". Suggested fix stands: gate cashPosition() and leverage() on debtReliable(L) the way roic()/roicValue() already do.
- **Suggested fix:** Gate cashPosition() and leverage() on debtReliable(L) exactly as roic()/roicValue() already do: when debt is under-captured, return a 'Debt under-captured — leverage unknown, not low' read (tone none) instead of a confident net-cash/Conservative figure. The guard already exists; wire it into the two solvency surfaces that currently bypass it so the 'Will it survive?' section stops contradicting itself.

### 6. [HIGH] Efficiency ratio produces false verdicts (>90% "Bloated" / <30% "Lean") for ~10% of banks when noninterestIncome/Expense is mis-tagged, with no sanity guard

- **Dimension / pools:** banks-fees · US, ADR/foreign
- **Principle:** A bank is read on operating efficiency; a verdict must be traceable and honest, and "present never pronounce" means never stamping a green/red label on a number that is arithmetic noise.
- **What is wrong:** efficiencyRatio (financials.mjs:30-34) computes noninterestExpense / ((NII||0)+(noninterestIncome||0)) and returns it for any rev>0 with no upper/lower bound. When the SEC facts API or an IFRS filer fails to cleanly tag the revenue components or the overhead line, the denominator or numerator collapses and the ratio explodes or vanishes — but the scorecard still renders a confident tone/label. Banks with null noninterestIncome get the denominator reduced to NII alone; foreign filers that dump nearly all opex outside the 'noninterestExpense' tag get an absurdly low ratio that hits the GREEN 'Lean' bucket.
- **Evidence:** UBS detail corrected: in fundamentals.adr.json it is netInterestIncome that is null (not noninterestIncome); noninterestIncome=30.581B, NIExp=565M → eff = 565/30581 = 1.8% → green 'Lean'. SCHW classifies as financialKind='bank' despite SIC 6211 (security broker), confirming it renders buildFinancialScorecard and receives the false 106.1% 'Bloated' verdict. Population: of 293 financial-archetype banks where efficiency computes, 14 >90% and 14 <30% (~10%), all artifacts of null/negative/mistagged income-statement components. Note the codebase already applies sanity guards to depositFunding and returnOnTangibleEquity, so the missing guard on efficiencyRatio is an inconsistency, not a documented design choice.
- **Suggested fix:** Guard the ratio: return null (show '—', not a verdict) when noninterestIncome or netInterestIncome is null/negative, or when the result falls outside a plausible band (e.g. ~20%–110%). Better, require both revenue components present and positive before computing, since a partially-tagged income statement cannot yield a comparable efficiency ratio.

### 7. [HIGH] Loss-ratio fallback has no believability guard, prints absurd ratios with verdict tones — including on Berkshire itself

- **Dimension / pools:** insurers-reits-care · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Present never pronounce; each metric shown with its honest limit; an insurer is read on underwriting
- **What is wrong:** combinedRatio() and medicalLossRatio() both clamp to a believable band (0.6–1.6 and 0.6–1.1) and return null when mis-tagged. But lossRatio() — the fallback shown whenever the all-in expense total is untagged — has NO such guard. It happily displays mis-mapped claims/premiums as a loss ratio with an ok/warn tone, on business models where a loss ratio is meaningless (mortgage insurers, title insurers, conglomerates). The displayed verdict ('ok'/'warn') tells a value investor the number is informative when it is garbage.
- **Evidence:** Verified exact figures. US: 70 P&C insurers (not 'P&C' headline 20 — 20 is the count that falls back to loss ratio; 10 of those 20 are out-of-band). BRK-A/BRK-B: loss ratio 108% 'warn' (claims 30,906M / premiums 28,746M; lossesAndExpenses null so combined ratio null). MTG 5% 'ok' (sic 6351), NMIH 10% 'ok', RDN 7% 'ok', FNF 9% 'ok' (sic 6361 title; its lossesAndExpenses 13,048M/2,795M=467% is why it falls back), PFG 126% 'warn' (sic 6321), plus ACIC 15%, HCI 29%, HRTG 39% all 'ok'. ADR: 14 P&C insurers, 0 get combined ratio, 13 fall to loss ratio: ESNT 2% 'ok', IGIC 17% 'ok', KWY 20% 'ok', MHLA/MHNC 373% 'warn', ACGLN/ACGLO/AXS 53% 'ok', GLRE 59%, JRVR 75%, KG 71%, PLGO 48%, SPNT 58% — all 'ok'/'warn' graded. All cited tickers route to subtype 'insurer' (P&C branch), confirmed via financialProfile(); none are life-insurers, so the loss-ratio fallback genuinely applies.
- **Suggested fix:** Apply the same believability band to lossRatio() that combinedRatio() and medicalLossRatio() already use (e.g. require ~0.4–0.95 for P&C), returning null (and the 'Not enough data' card) outside it. At minimum, drop the ok/warn tone to neutral 'info' for the loss-ratio fallback so it is never presented as a graded verdict.

### 8. [MEDIUM] Displayed span and year count overstate the depth of the owner-earnings record when early years lack capex

- **Dimension / pools:** normalization · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Graham: a through-cycle figure must actually span the cycle; honest disclosure of the window used
- **What is wrong:** The gate is oem.length>=4 (after dropping years with null/implausible OE margin), but the rendered span is built from the full revenue history (H[0].fy–H[last].fy) and years = max(oem.length, nm.length). So a company that only has capex (hence owner-earnings) for its recent boom years still displays a long span and a large year count, implying the normalized margin was averaged over the full window when it was actually computed from only the recent, non-representative years. A reader trusting a '10-year through-cycle median' is being shown a figure built from far fewer years — and specifically the boom years.
- **Evidence:** NVDA (US pool): capex null FY2017–2021, present FY2022–2026. OE-margin series = [null×5, 30.2, 14.1, 44.4, 47.7, 46.3]%. earningsPower() returns normOeMargin=44.35% (median of the 5 present years), oemRange 14.1–47.7%, latestOeMargin=48.29%, cyclePos="at". But ep.span="2017–2026" and ep.years=10. NormalizedEarnings.astro renders the lede "earns on its through-cycle margin across 2017–2026" and the read "look representative of normal power, not a peak or a trough" — both built from a median resting only on the 5 AI-boom years. Note: ep.years is computed but not displayed in this component; the actual rendered overstatement is the span text and the boom-only cyclePos="at" verdict. Broader US-pool scan finds 13 affected companies (e.g. CUZ, EXR, LGIH oeYears=4 vs years=10).
- **Suggested fix:** Build span and the displayed year count from the SAME series used for the normalized owner-earnings median (the plausible oem years), not the revenue history or net-margin count. State 'median of N owner-earnings years' explicitly, and consider withholding/flagging when the OE years cluster only in recent boom years.

### 9. [LOW] ROIC is computed on banks and insurers in the lib (meaningless debt+equity−cash base); only the render layer saves it, leaving a fragile gate

- **Dimension / pools:** moat-roic · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Munger/Buffett: a bank is a balance-sheet business read on book value, net interest margin and credit losses, not on an invested-capital return; debt (deposits/wholesale funding) is its raw material, not capital invested in operations.
- **What is wrong:** roicValue() and roic() have no financialKind guard. For a bank, invested = totalDebt + equity − cash treats hundreds of billions of funding as invested capital and divides operating income by it, yielding a number that is neither ROIC nor ROE. The page is currently protected only because Scorecard.astro nulls moatReport for financials and TenYear.astro uses a curated row set that omits the ROIC row — both at the call site, not in the metric. Any future surface that calls roicValue/roic without re-checking financialKind will print a bank ROIC.
- **Evidence:** roicValue() reproduced exactly as claimed (latest-year): JPM 23%, BAC 20%, C 21%, GS 22%, SCHW 55%, MET 15% (insurer); also WFC 14%, AIG 5%. roic() headline uses the through-cycle median so its value differs from roicValue (JPM 10% "Solid through the cycle", SCHW 28% "Exceptional through the cycle", BAC/C/WFC "Below average through the cycle") — both confirmed as numbers built on debt+equity−cash with no financialKind guard. Correction to the finding: it is NOT true that only Scorecard.astro and TenYear.astro protect the page. Every ROIC consumer gates at the call site via financialKind branches — Portrait.astro:91 and Peers.astro:111 each place the ROIC row/column inside the non-financial archetype branch, and BusinessRead/MoatUnderAI/concepts are moat or definitional surfaces. No bank/insurer ROIC currently reaches a user, which is why this is a latent fragility issue (low) rather than a live mispresentation (medium).
- **Suggested fix:** Make roicValue()/roic() return null / 'not meaningful here' when financialKind(company) is a bank or insurer, mirroring the existing oiReliable gate, so the guard lives in the metric rather than in every consumer.

### 10. [LOW] Bank-side returnOnEquity has no positive-equity guard, unlike the fee-side feeReturnOnEquity, so negative equity can yield a positive 'Exceptional' ROE

- **Dimension / pools:** banks-fees · US, ADR/foreign
- **Principle:** Guards against ratios that 'explode or flip sign and stop meaning anything' must be applied consistently; the fee module already documents and guards this exact trap.
- **What is wrong:** returnOnEquity (financials.mjs:13-14) uses `L.stockholdersEquity` as a mere truthy check, so negative equity passes through. With negative net income AND negative equity, the ratio is positive and the tone logic (roe<0?'bad'... else high buckets) would label it 'Strong'/'Exceptional'. feeReturnOnEquity (feeFinancials.mjs:20-23) explicitly guards `stockholdersEquity > 0` for precisely this reason, so the two modules treat the same hazard differently.
- **Evidence:** Code asymmetry confirmed (financials.mjs:13-14 unguarded vs feeFinancials.mjs:20-23 guarded). Reproduced sign flip: NI=-50/SE=-100 -> +0.5 ROE -> tone "good", label "Exceptional" via financials.mjs:61-62. The latest-period scorecard label is latent: no kind:"bank" company has negative latest stockholdersEquity in US/ADR/JP pools. HOWEVER, financialQuality (the brief) applies the same unguarded ratio over .history and fires TODAY: SOFI (kind=bank, sic 6199) shows 2019 ROE +70.7% (NI -239.7M / SE -339.1M) and 2020 ROE +186.5% (NI -224.1M / SE -120.1M) — two losses rendered as exceptional returns; current brief "median 5%, above 12% in 2 of 7 years" becomes median -6%, 0 of 5 with a >0 guard. ATLC (kind=bank, sic 6141) shows 2017 ROE +113.2% from a $40.8M loss (NI -40.8M / SE -36.0M). Suggested fix correct for returnOnEquity/financialQuality; but the fix's inclusion of returnOnAssets/equityToAssets is unnecessary — those do not sign-flip (assets positive; negative equity in equityToAssets correctly reads "Thin"/bad).
- **Suggested fix:** Mirror the fee-side guard: require stockholdersEquity > 0 in returnOnEquity (and in returnOnAssets/equityToAssets where a sign flip would mislead), returning null so the scorecard shows '—' rather than a flattering verdict.

## Theme C. Normalization & turnaround handling

_5 finding(s): 2 high, 1 medium, 2 low._

### 11. [HIGH] Normalized owner earnings go NEGATIVE and the section pronounces a record-profit year a 'lean trough that understates the business' — flatly wrong for turnarounds

- **Dimension / pools:** normalization · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Graham: normalized earning power across a cycle; 'present, never pronounce'
- **What is wrong:** earningsPower takes the median owner-earnings margin across the whole record, including the deep loss years of a structural turnaround. For a business that crossed from chronic losses into profit, the median is negative, so the headline 'Normalized owner earnings' is shown as a negative number and framed as the business's normal earning power. Worse, cyclePos compares the (positive) latest margin to the (negative) median, computes ratio<0.85, labels it 'below', and the rendered read tells the reader the latest figure 'may understate the business; the reported figure is being set in a lean year.' For a turnaround at its best-ever year this is exactly backwards: it pronounces a recovering business permanently unprofitable and calls its record year a trough. This is a verdict, and a wrong one — violating 'present, never pronounce'.
- **Evidence:** ALNY (US, span 2016-2025): full OE margins per year = -684.6, -440.6, -771.4, -134.5, -131.8, -81.6, -56.5, +2.3, -1.9, +12.5 (%). The plausible filter (|v|<=1.0) drops the first five (>100% magnitude), leaving 5 values [-81.6, -56.5, -1.9, +2.3, +12.5]%; median = -1.89% -> normOeMargin=-1.89%, normOE=-$70.4M (the 'Normalized owner earnings' headline). latestOeMargin=+12.53%, latestOE=+$465.4M (best year). cyclePos='below' because ratio = 12.53%/-1.89% = -6.6 < 0.85 (no sign guard at normalize.mjs:49-51). Rendered read (NormalizedEarnings.astro:20): '...the latest owner earnings may understate the business; the reported figure is being set in a lean year.' Scale (recomputed): US 99 inverted + 228 negative-headline; ADR 47 inverted + 125 negative-headline. Additional confirmed inverted cases: AFRM (norm -$399M / latest +$787M @ +57.73%), ACAD (norm -$264M / latest +$123M @ +11.19%). Note: the finding's per-year list and ALNY normOE/normOeMargin/latest figures are accurate; the median is driven by the 5 surviving post-filter values, not all 10.
- **Suggested fix:** Withhold the normalized owner-earnings figure (or show only the margin range, not a normalized $ headline and not a cyclePos read) when the median margin is negative or when the OE-margin series crosses zero across the record — those are structural turnarounds/loss-makers, not businesses with a cyclically-averaged earning power. At minimum, never render the 'lean year / understates' read when normOeMargin<=0.

### 12. [HIGH] 'Wide swing: a cyclical, read it on the average' label fires on non-cyclicals because swing divides by a near-zero median

- **Dimension / pools:** normalization · US, ADR
- **Principle:** Graham: distinguish a real cycle from a one-way structural change; do not mislabel
- **What is wrong:** swing = (max margin − min margin) / |median margin|. When the through-cycle median margin sits near zero, swing explodes regardless of whether the business is actually cyclical. The component fires the label 'a wide swing: a cyclical, read it on the average' whenever swing>1.2. So any business whose margin merely crossed zero once (a turnaround, a one-time impairment year, a young company) is told the reader to value it on its (often negative/near-zero) average. This both mislabels the business type and steers the reader toward the bad normalized figure from finding 1.
- **Evidence:** src/lib/normalize.mjs:54 computes swing against |median OE margin| (the comment says 'mean' but the divisor is normOeMargin = median(oem) from line 32 — minor comment inaccuracy). Label fires in src/components/NormalizedEarnings.astro:24,49. Reproduced: ALNY swing=49.71 (median -1.89%, range -81.6% to 12.5%), ADTN swing=12.90 (median -1.37%, range -8.6% to 9.0%) — both labeled 'a cyclical.' Genuine cyclicals score lower: XOM=2.34 (median 6.65%), MU=4.11 (median 19.70%); AAPL=0.23 (not labeled). ### 27. [HIGH] ADR reverse-DCF divides a USD price by home-currency owner earnings and a home-currency (undivided) share count — multiple is off by FX × ADR-ratio, and the verdict inverts

The tool tells the reader to type 'Today's price ... read it off any broker', which for a US reader is the NYSE/NASDAQ ADR quoted in USD. The script then computes marketCap = priceUSD × SH (home ordinary shares) and feeds mc/base into solve(), and base = cashFromOps − |capex| in the home currency. So it multiplies a USD price by a TWD/JPY share count and divides by TWD/JPY cash flow.
