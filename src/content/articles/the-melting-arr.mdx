*Working notes — outline to be written in R.G.'s voice. This launches the
standing beat; it is a ledger to be kept, not a top to be called.*

- The underwriting sentence: 2020–22 software buyouts were levered on ARR —
  sometimes literally ARR loans, not EBITDA loans — assuming 110%+ net revenue
  retention, near-zero churn, forever.
- The mechanism (sharper than "AI causes layoffs"): the lender doesn't need the
  layoffs to happen. Seats compress when agents do work humans did; vendors
  pre-emptively reprice from seats to consumption; and every corporate finance
  team that merely *opens* a build-vs-buy analysis walks into renewal with
  leverage. Planned savings can under-deliver and the credit still breaks —
  collateral modeled as a bond, behaving as a melting ice cube, while PIK
  accrues. PIK is the tell: paying lenders later, from a future that's
  shrinking.
- The kill zone: levered mediocrity, not category kings. Nobody hands a finance
  graduate an AI assistant and says rebuild the ERP; everyone rebuilds the
  $400k-a-year workflow tool. Systems of record buy years with accountability,
  compliance, and data gravity. The build-vs-buy line moved; it didn't vanish.
- The receipts as of mid-2026 (re-verify all at publication): Fitch private
  credit defaults at a record ~9.2%; "true" default rate near 5% counting
  liability-management exercises vs. under 2% headline; mid-market software
  around 40% of some private loan portfolios; software the largest PIK sector;
  UBS aggressive-AI scenario at ~13% defaults; FSB vulnerabilities report, May
  2026. Case study #1: Pluralsight, 2024 — equity to zero, lenders took the
  keys, on exactly this structure.
- The irony paragraph: private credit is financing both sides — the AI
  data-center buildout and the legacy software paper the buildout erodes.
  Lending the disruptor the money to melt its own collateral.
- The teachable skill (the beat's recurring engine): **how to read a BDC
  quarterly filing.** Private credit is opaque, but BDCs publicly file
  name-by-name schedules of investments, marks, and PIK flags on EDGAR every
  quarter. Walk readers through one, line by line.
- Standing instrumentation, updated as it prints: software marks and PIK share
  across major BDCs; NRR trends in public SaaS cohorts; seat-to-consumption
  pricing announcements; amend-and-extends; AI-savings announcements paired with
  realized opex twelve months later.
- Pre-registered falsifiers, published with the kickoff: NRR stabilizing or
  rising; refis clearing at par; PIK share declining; realized AI savings
  failing to appear in opex. If those print, the thesis is wrong and the beat
  says so.
