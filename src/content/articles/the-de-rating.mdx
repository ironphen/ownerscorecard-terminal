source conversation: these figures came from scanning the financials, not from
reading the reports. Read the last three 10-Ks before publishing.*

- The arithmetic that opens the piece (verify exact figures): roughly $700 in
  November 2021; under $210 in June 2026 after-hours. Over the same stretch,
  revenue per share grew from about $33 (FY21) to about $55.80 (FY25); EPS from
  $10.02 to $16.70. Earnings up two-thirds; price down two-thirds. **Every
  dollar of the decline came from the multiple** — roughly 70x to roughly 12.6x.
- The mechanism to teach: price equals earnings times a multiple, and the
  multiple is not a mood — it is a *duration-confidence claim*. Most of a growth
  stock's discounted value lives past year ten. AI didn't cut Adobe's earnings;
  it cut the market's confidence interval on year-twenty earnings, where the
  value lived. The market isn't necessarily saying the business is bad — it's
  saying it no longer knows how long the business lasts.
- The honest admission (the part that makes this piece matter): it is no longer
  possible to hold a 50-year circle of competence in application software. Tools
  as engrained as Excel, PowerPoint, and the Adobe suite — load-bearing for
  entire professions and school systems — are simultaneously everywhere and
  immediately questionable. Graham's framework survives this *because it never
  required owning such things*: the growing too-hard basket is a finding, not a
  failure (Munger's three baskets: in, out, too hard).
- The canonical pair for the circle-of-competence-in-tech section: IBM (Buffett
  breaking his own too-hard rule in 2011, by his own later admission) vs. Apple
  (underwritten as a consumer franchise with switching costs, not as
  technology).
- The open question the piece must NOT resolve casually: at ~12.6x earnings the
  market is pricing something close to terminal decline. Melting ice cube, or
  misunderstood toll bridge? That is a handicapping question answerable only by
  reading the filings — which is the firm's actual work. Candidate items to
  check: net new ARR trend, Firefly/AI attach, pricing actions vs seat counts,
  and the buyback record (how much was repurchased at 30–50x that now trades at
  12x — capital allocation as part of the story).
- Doctrine check: no verdict on the stock. The piece teaches de-rating as a
  mechanism every reader will meet again, with Adobe as this decade's cleanest
  specimen.
