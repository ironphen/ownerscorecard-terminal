*Working notes — outline to be written in R.G.'s voice. This is the prototype of
the format: an explainer that teaches a mechanism, with its checker embedded so
the reader can immediately apply it. The tool also stands alone at `/tools/coverage`.*

- Open with the reframe: before "is it cheap?" comes "will it be here?" Solvency
  is the question under all the others, and it has a number.
- Teach the mechanism plainly: operating income ÷ interest expense = times
  interest earned. Below 1×, a full year of operating profit didn't cover the
  interest bill — the gap was filled by refinancing, asset sales, or a lender's
  patience. That's the zombie definition, in one line a reader can compute.
- Embed the tool right here, so reading becomes doing:

<CoverageChecker client:visible />

- Then walk one example from the tool in prose — pick a thin one and a
  comfortable one, and show what the ratio is and isn't telling you.
- The honest boundary (doctrine: no verdicts): coverage rules a company *out* or
  keeps it *in the game*. It never says "buy." Solvent is not cheap. Say so.
- Tie to the beat: this is the same ratio that turns "AI is pressuring software"
  from a vibe into a check — run it on a levered, sponsor-owned software name and
  watch the margin of safety thin out (links to "The Melting ARR").
- Close on the method, not the names: you don't need our opinion. The filing is
  public, the arithmetic is two lines, and the tool links you to the source. The
  ability to check is the whole point.
