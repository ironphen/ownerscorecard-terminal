*Working notes — outline to be written in R.G.'s voice. News peg: SpaceX priced
June 11, 2026, debuted June 12. The peg expires; the mechanism doesn't.*

- The 1949 rule, stated plainly: new issues come to market at a moment chosen by
  the seller, under conditions favorable to the seller. Then simply describe
  this deal and let the reader feel the rule working.
- The facts (sourced at publication — CNBC, S-1, Morningstar): fixed $135 price
  implying ~$1.75T — 61% above the company's own December 2025 private mark;
  ~$250B of demand against a $75B raise; **retail deliberately earmarked 30% of
  the float, about triple the mega-cap norm**; Q1 2026 net loss $4.28B;
  accumulated deficit $41.3B; ~94x trailing revenue; Morningstar's published
  fair value under half the target.
- The allocation question (the horse arrives at the water): in a deal four times
  oversubscribed, allocation is a choice. Why would the most sophisticated
  capital-raising operation on earth choose to hand the largest-ever share to
  the least price-sensitive bidders? Don't answer it. Ask it.
- The decade math: for a $135 buyer to earn 10% a year for ten years, the
  company must be worth roughly $4.5T by 2036; at a generous 30x that requires
  ~$150B of annual net income — about seven Walmarts of profit — starting from a
  ~$17B annual loss run-rate. The price already contains the miracle.
- The disarming sentence, load-bearing: none of this says SpaceX is a bad
  company. It may be the most consequential engineering organization alive. A
  great company and a great stock are different objects — the only thing Graham
  ever asked anyone to learn.
- The empathy turn (the site's signature register): the $70B retail bid is not
  stupidity. It is a generation priced out of houses being marketed a lottery
  ticket as the remedy. The system that caps the paycheck is selling the dream
  back at 94x revenue. Recruit the bidder; never mock them.
- Doctrine check: no top-calling, no Musk. The piece must survive
  find-and-replace on the company name.
