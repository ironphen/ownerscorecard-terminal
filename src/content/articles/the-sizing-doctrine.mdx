falsifiable page the rest of the site points at. Publish it before the next
drawdown, not after.*

- **The dial: extrinsic share of premium.** Every option dollar splits into
  intrinsic (financed ownership — equity exposure with non-callable, defined-risk
  leverage) and extrinsic (rented time — a paid bet on *when*). Deep ITM Jan-27
  calls on a stable business: mostly intrinsic. OTM calls on a meme stock: 100%
  extrinsic. The glide path is this number falling as capital grows — deeper in
  the money, further out in expiry. Chart it quarterly. Watch it fall.
- **The glide is self-funding**: deep ITM costs more premium per idea, which is
  exactly why it wasn't affordable at $15k and increasingly is now. Each step of
  wealth makes the safer expression of the same idea affordable.
- **Liability trigger, not wealth trigger.** "I still need more" never fires —
  needs are a moving target. The rule: the day this account acquires a date
  (down payment, business launch), the extrinsic sleeve shrinks to what that
  date can survive. Until then, the stake must stay rebuildable by salary.
- **Refill on payday, never on tilt.** Graham's no-refill rule, translated for
  the accumulation phase: contributions to the speculative sleeve are
  calendar-driven, at the budgeted rate, decided by the version of me that wrote
  the budget — never event-driven top-ups after a loss or a win.
- **Residue is budgeted, not denied.** The tell is never the first speculative
  ticket — it's the event-driven refills that follow it: a second lot at a
  higher strike after the first goes against you, a third bought near expiry to
  "make it back." A hard annual residue cap with the no-refill rule forbids
  exactly those.
- **Sequencing**: the day job is the load-bearing column — it's what makes the
  stake rebuildable. The glide completes before the job goes.
