*Working notes — outline to be written in R.G.'s voice.*

- The under-discussed frame: before any technique, Graham sorts the reader.
  Expected return tracks *intelligent effort*, not risk appetite. And his
  sternest warning: the middle ground — casual stock-picking for a little extra
  return — is the worst seat at the table. Commit or index. That warning is
  *more* true with a casino in every pocket: Graham's dabbler had to phone a
  broker; today's gets the temptation as a push notification.
- The defensive investor, 2026: solved (see "1976"). Say it with respect.
- The enterprising investor, 2026 — the honest edge taxonomy:
  1. **Size** — you can fill positions in things institutions can't touch.
     Buffett's best returns were his smallest sums; the 2004 Korean manual is
     the canonical story.
  2. **Horizon** — no quarterly career risk, no redemptions.
  3. **Expression** — the convexity toolkit (listed options barely existed when
     Graham wrote; LEAPS arrived 1990), governed by the sizing doctrine.
  4. **Structural flows** — see the symbiosis point below.
- The symbiosis thesis (the essay's original contribution): **the defensive
  crowd's dominance manufactures the enterprising crowd's opportunities.** When
  every marginal dollar buys the cap-weighted top, what falls out the bottom:
  uncovered small caps, spinoffs index funds must sell regardless of price,
  mechanical deletions, anything foreign or odd or unscreenable. Graham's
  enterprising investor competed with everyone reading the same manuals;
  today's competes with almost nobody below a couple billion of market cap.
- The honest non-edges, equally explicit: information, speed, screens, macro.
  Anything screenable is arbitraged precisely because everyone got Graham's
  tools for free.
- Close with the gate: the effort bar, stated plainly. Below it, index happily
  and read this site for the mechanisms. Above it, welcome — bring receipts.
